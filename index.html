Gold Loan Credit Risk Prediction

Problem Statement

Assessing borrower risk in gold loan portfolios is critical for financial institutions to minimize defaults and improve lending decisions. A structured approach is required to identify high-risk borrowers and improve credit decision accuracy.

Dataset Used

A synthetic dataset generated using the Faker library in Python, simulating real-world gold loan data such as customer demographics, loan amount, repayment behavior, and risk indicators. This enabled controlled experimentation and model development.

Methodology

Data preprocessing was carried out using Excel and Python (Pandas, NumPy), including handling missing values, feature engineering, and data transformation. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was performed to identify patterns and relationships affecting credit risk.

Models & Techniques

Implemented Random Forest Classifier and XGBoost Classifier for credit risk prediction. Used Matplotlib and Seaborn for visualization and feature analysis. Model performance was evaluated using accuracy and comparative analysis..

Results & Insights

The Random Forest model achieved 90.87% accuracy, outperforming XGBoost (90.10%), and was selected as the final model. The model successfully identified key risk drivers and improved classification of high-risk borrowers, supporting better lending decisions..

Global Data Center Risk Scoring

Problem Statement

With the rapid global expansion of digital infrastructure, understanding where data centers are concentrated, which countries are investing in new facilities, and which companies dominate the market is important for investors, policymakers, and technology planners. A structured analysis is needed to track global data-center distribution and identify emerging growth regions.

Dataset Used

Open datasets sourced from Kaggle containing global data-center information, including number of facilities by country, major companies operating data centers, and records of upcoming or planned data-center projects.

Methodology

Data was cleaned and transformed using Excel, Power Query, and Python (Pandas). Country-level aggregations were performed to calculate the total number of data centers, upcoming projects, and company ownership distribution. The processed data was visualised in interactive Power BI dashboards for geographic and market analysis..

Models & Techniques

Data cleaning and transformation, aggregation analysis, trend analysis, and interactive dashboard visualisation. Python was used for preprocessing and exploratory analysis, while Power BI was used to create geographic maps, comparative charts, and company distribution visuals.

Results & Insights

The dashboard provides a global overview of data-center distribution by country, highlights regions with the highest number of upcoming projects, and identifies the top companies operating data centers worldwide. The analysis reveals major infrastructure hubs and emerging markets where data-center investments are growing rapidly.

Used Car Price Prediction

Problem Statement

The used-car market suffers from information asymmetry. Buyers and sellers lack a data-driven benchmark for fair pricing, leading to suboptimal transactions. This project aims to build a reliable price prediction engine.

Dataset Used

A publicly available used-car dataset containing features such as make, model, year, mileage, fuel type, transmission, and seller type across thousands of listings.

Methodology

Comprehensive EDA using Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn revealed key pricing drivers. Categorical encoding, outlier removal, and feature scaling were applied before modelling.

Models & Techniques

Multiple Linear Regression (Scikit-learn) was the primary model, with feature importance analysis and cross-validation to guard against overfitting.

Results & Insights

Achieved strong predictive accuracy (R² score reported). Mileage and vehicle age were the dominant price drivers. The model correctly identified premium-brand price premiums that were not apparent in raw data.
